# GEO

## Scope

This file covers report-backed Generative Engine Optimization practices.

## Rules

### 1) Keep helpful, original, non-commodity content as the baseline
- **Rule name:** Helpful content baseline
- **Description:** Google says generative AI optimization still follows SEO fundamentals, including valuable, non-commodity content.
- **Source URL:** https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide
- **Source organization:** Google Search Central
- **Source type:** Official documentation
- **Classification:** Official Standard
- **Severity:** High
- **Confidence level:** High
- **Evidence strength:** Strong
- **Why it matters:** The report treats helpful content as the foundation for both search and AI visibility.
- **Expected impact:** Better overall search alignment.
- **Implementation guidance:** Build pages that actually help the audience rather than repeating generic information.
- **Example:** An original guide with concrete details and examples.
- **Exceptions:** None in the report.
- **Potential drawbacks:** None beyond editorial effort.
- **Conflicting opinions:** The report notes that GEO is still SEO from Google’s perspective.
- **Notes:** This is the baseline rule under all GEO practices.

### 2) Lead with direct answers before supporting detail
- **Rule name:** Answer-first structure
- **Description:** Put the answer or key fact early, ideally under clear headings.
- **Source URL:** https://www.lumar.io/blog/best-practice/4-pillar-geo-strategy-framework-for-ai-search-visibility/
- **Source organization:** Lumar
- **Source type:** Industry analysis
- **Classification:** Emerging GEO Practice
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** Medium
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate
- **Why it matters:** The report says this may improve AI extractability.
- **Expected impact:** Better chance of AI using the page as a source.
- **Implementation guidance:** Open sections with concise direct answers.
- **Example:** `What is SEO? SEO is...`
- **Exceptions:** Pages that need a different narrative structure.
- **Potential drawbacks:** Can feel repetitive if overused.
- **Conflicting opinions:** Google does not formally require answer-first prose.
- **Notes:** Promising but not standardized.

### 3) Use entity clarity instead of ambiguous pronouns
- **Rule name:** Entity clarity
- **Description:** Refer to the main entity by name rather than relying on vague pronouns.
- **Source URL:** https://www.lumar.io/blog/best-practice/4-pillar-geo-strategy-framework-for-ai-search-visibility/
- **Source organization:** Lumar
- **Source type:** Industry analysis
- **Classification:** Emerging GEO Practice
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** Medium
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate
- **Why it matters:** The report says this helps AI systems connect passages to the right entity.
- **Expected impact:** Better entity recognition and extractability.
- **Implementation guidance:** Repeat the brand/product/topic name when needed for clarity.
- **Example:** Use `Acme Shoes` instead of repeated `they` or `it`.
- **Exceptions:** Natural language still applies.
- **Potential drawbacks:** Repetition can become awkward.
- **Conflicting opinions:** None in the report.
- **Notes:** This is one of the report’s central GEO ideas.

### 4) Break long content into retrievable chunks
- **Rule name:** Content chunking
- **Description:** Use clear H2/H3 sections to divide a page into coherent passages.
- **Source URL:** https://www.lumar.io/blog/best-practice/4-pillar-geo-strategy-framework-for-ai-search-visibility/
- **Source organization:** Lumar
- **Source type:** Industry analysis
- **Classification:** Emerging GEO Practice
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** Medium
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate
- **Why it matters:** The report says AI retrieval may work better on clear content blocks.
- **Expected impact:** Higher extractability.
- **Implementation guidance:** Separate definitions, steps, and comparisons into distinct sections.
- **Example:** `What causes acne?`, `Topical treatments`, `Lifestyle tips`.
- **Exceptions:** Very short pages do not need heavy chunking.
- **Potential drawbacks:** Over-fragmentation can hurt flow.
- **Conflicting opinions:** None in the report.
- **Notes:** This is an emerging GEO pattern.

### 8) Do not treat llms.txt as a Google Search visibility lever
- **Rule name:** llms.txt limitation
- **Description:** The report says Google clarified that llms.txt has no effect on Google Search visibility.
- **Source URL:** https://developers.google.com/search/updates#removing-faq-rich-result
- **Source organization:** Google Search Central
- **Source type:** Official documentation update
- **Classification:** Experimental
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** High
- **Evidence strength:** Strong
- **Why it matters:** It prevents wasting effort on a file that does not help Google Search visibility.
- **Expected impact:** More focused optimization effort.
- **Implementation guidance:** Do not substitute llms.txt for standard SEO work.
- **Example:** Keep focus on content, metadata, and structure.
- **Exceptions:** The report notes possible third-party AI use, but not Google Search benefit.
- **Potential drawbacks:** None.
- **Conflicting opinions:** None in the report.
- **Notes:** This is explicit in the report.
