# AI Search

## Scope

This file covers AI Overviews, AI search experiences, and the report’s AI visibility guidance.

## Rules

### 1) Treat GEO/AEO as an extension of SEO fundamentals
- **Rule name:** SEO baseline for AI search
- **Description:** Google’s report-backed position is that optimizing for generative AI search is still SEO.
- **Source URL:** https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide
- **Source organization:** Google Search Central
- **Source type:** Official documentation
- **Classification:** Official Standard
- **Severity:** High
- **Confidence level:** High
- **Evidence strength:** Strong
- **Why it matters:** It prevents treating AI search as a separate discipline detached from standard SEO.
- **Expected impact:** Keeps optimization grounded in official guidance.
- **Implementation guidance:** Continue focusing on helpful content, crawlability, metadata, and structured data.
- **Example:** A well-structured article with source-backed claims.
- **Exceptions:** None in the report.
- **Potential drawbacks:** None beyond misunderstanding the term “GEO.”
- **Conflicting opinions:** Google explicitly frames GEO/AEO as SEO from its perspective.
- **Notes:** This is one of the most important report conclusions.

### 2) Optimize for clarity, structure, and evidence in AI-facing content
- **Rule name:** AI-visible content quality
- **Description:** AI-oriented pages should be clear, factual, answer-focused, and supported by sources.
- **Source URL:** https://www.lumar.io/blog/best-practice/4-pillar-geo-strategy-framework-for-ai-search-visibility/
- **Source organization:** Lumar
- **Source type:** Industry analysis
- **Classification:** Emerging GEO Practice
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** Medium
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate
- **Why it matters:** The report treats these as promising for AI answer visibility.
- **Expected impact:** Better AI citation potential.
- **Implementation guidance:** Keep content accurate, answer-oriented, and easy to scan.
- **Example:** A comparison page with direct conclusions and source notes.
- **Exceptions:** Artistic or opinion-heavy content may not fit the pattern.
- **Potential drawbacks:** Over-structuring can feel formulaic.
- **Conflicting opinions:** None in the report.
- **Notes:** This overlaps with GEO and AEO guidance.

### 3) Maintain strong brand/entity presence online
- **Rule name:** Brand presence
- **Description:** The report recommends consistent entity signals across the site and across the web.
- **Source URL:** https://www.lumar.io/blog/best-practice/4-pillar-geo-strategy-framework-for-ai-search-visibility/
- **Source organization:** Lumar
- **Source type:** Industry analysis
- **Classification:** Emerging GEO Practice
- **Severity:** Opportunity
- **Confidence level:** Medium
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate
- **Why it matters:** Consistent identity helps recognition in AI-assisted search experiences.
- **Expected impact:** Better brand recognition and trust.
- **Implementation guidance:** Use Organization schema and consistent brand naming.
- **Example:** Homepage, about page, and social profiles all match.
- **Exceptions:** Small sites can keep it simpler.
- **Potential drawbacks:** Bad entity data can confuse systems.
- **Conflicting opinions:** None in the report.
- **Notes:** This is a visibility and trust rule, not a formal ranking guarantee.
